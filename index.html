About

I am a Professor of Economics at the Department of Economics, University of Gothenburg. I also hold a position as Researcher at the Research Institute of Industrial Economics (IFN) in Stockholm.

My research is empirically oriented with a main focus on political, public and labor economics. I combine modern causal inference methods with Swedish register data and historical data to address questions of both academic and policy relevance. My work spans topics including the effects of political institutions on economic development, education policy, discrimination, crime and sentencing, and the quality of public vs. private service provision.

In addition to publishing in peer-reviewed journals such as Econometrica and The Quarterly Journal of Economics, I engage in policy-relevant work including government inquiries, reports, and public debate articles.
